Euclid's Algorithm

Euclid's algorithm is used to determine the greatest common divisor of two numbers. It does this by taking the remainder of the quotient of the larger number divided by the smaller number, and repeatedly swapping the quotient of this equation with the divisor of the equation before it until the remainder is zero, at which point the divisor is the greatest common divisor of the two original numbers.

This site's purpose is to demonstrate this idea visually as opposed to just supplying an answer to the equation.
